Part One · Project Overview

The Mission

Domain context

Fidessa runs two trading platforms in parallel — a legacy system built over thirty years ago and a newer platform a decade in the making.

Full migration is projected to take ten or more years. Maintaining both codebases consumes development capacity that would otherwise go toward the modern platform.

To accelerate the transition, Fidessa embeds legacy tools directly into the new platform so users can migrate without losing existing workflows. The two platforms differ significantly in tooling, UI, and interaction model. No one had mapped what that friction looked like in practice. I did. That meant domain research across both platforms, advocating against a fixed deadline, and designing disclosure for what couldn't be fixed in time.

Part One · Project Overview

The Problem

Stated vs. actual

Stated ask

Capture usability concerns introduced by running the legacy and new platforms side-by-side, and design solutions before the February 2026 release.

Actual problem

I found that the migration plan had overlooked US segment users entirely — leaving a mixed setup where the new platform covered only ~50% of their regional workflows.

The original plan was to ship with seven known concerns unresolved and conduct usability testing post-launch. For a system serving 6,500+ global financial firms, disrupting live trading is a revenue risk — not a usability inconvenience.

9 months · 4 phases

I made the case through domain knowledge alone. I got development reprioritized, addressed every resolvable concern before launch, and designed disclosure for the two that couldn't be fixed before users encountered them.

Part Three · Advocate

The Decision

Two paths to the PM

Every bike shop was closed. I fixed it anyway.

The original plan was to ship with known concerns and address them post-launch. I pushed back. The concerns were more disruptive than the plan assumed, and there was no realistic dev bandwidth to fix them later either.

Option 1 · Ship as planned

Hit the February 2026 deadline. Conduct usability testing to document the disruption. Use the results to make the case to stakeholders post-launch.

Option 2 · Rescope

Address the highest-impact blockers before forcing 3M users to migrate. Delay resolves risk rather than defers it.

No one asked me to audit the migration. I saw the risk, started investigating, and brought the findings to the PM with a clear recommendation. That investigation changed the launch plan.

Making the Case

Domain reasoning, no test data

The harder task was advocating internally for dev resources before launch. I reframed each concern as a revenue risk and ranked them by impact to give the PM a prioritized, actionable case rather than a list of UX complaints.

Part Four · Design

Onboarding Flow

Setting expectations before users hit them

I designed the onboarding announcement to get ahead of the two concerns we couldn't resolve. Users knew what to expect before they encountered them.

Content covered

  • The mixed tool environment and what it means for desktop organization
  • New alert functionality and where alerts now surface
  • Location of legacy toolbar in the new environment
  • Notice about previously grouped or tabbed tools
  • Theming acknowledged transparently — committed for future release

What Shipped

Feb 2026 · Gradual rollout

A forced migration of 3M users, rolled out gradually across 6,500+ global firms tied to each client's update cycle. Five of seven concerns resolved before go-live. Two disclosed proactively with committed resolution dates.
